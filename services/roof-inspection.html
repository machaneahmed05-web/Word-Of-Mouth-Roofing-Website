Comprehensive Assessment

We'll Give You All The Details

Our certified roof inspectors conduct a thorough, multi-point assessment of every component of your roofing system. We don't just look at the shingles — we evaluate your entire roof from top to bottom, inside and out.

  • Shingle condition — cracking, curling, granule loss, missing pieces
  • Flashing integrity around chimneys, vents, and valleys
  • Gutter condition and drainage effectiveness
  • Soffit, fascia, and eave inspection
  • Attic ventilation and insulation assessment
  • Structural integrity — decking, rafters, ridgeline
  • Moisture damage, mold, and rot detection
  • Leak source identification
  • Storm and hail damage evaluation

After the inspection, you receive a detailed written report with photos documenting every finding. We'll walk you through our findings in plain language and give you our honest recommendation — repair, maintenance, or replacement — with zero pressure.

Inspection Scenarios

When To Schedule A Roof Inspection

After A Storm

Hail and high winds cause damage that isn't always visible from the ground. Schedule an inspection after any significant weather event to document damage for your insurance claim.

Before Buying A Home

A roof inspection before closing can reveal hidden issues that affect your offer price or negotiation. Don't buy a home without knowing the condition of the roof above you.

Before Selling A Home

A clean inspection report builds buyer confidence and can increase your home's sale price. Identify and address issues before they become deal-breakers during escrow.

Annual Maintenance Check

Even without obvious damage, an annual inspection catches minor wear before it escalates. Most roofing professionals recommend inspecting twice a year — spring and fall.

Insurance Claim Support

We work directly with your insurance carrier and provide the documentation needed to support your claim. Our inspection reports are insurance-ready and accepted by all major carriers.

Roof Is 10+ Years Old

Roofs over a decade old should be inspected regularly. Catching deterioration early extends your roof's life and helps you plan financially for eventual replacement.
