Making the Right Call

When To Repair vs. Replace Your Roof

Not every roofing problem requires a full replacement — but knowing when repair is no longer enough can save you thousands of dollars in interior damage down the road. Word of Mouth Roofing gives you an honest recommendation, never upselling you on work you don't need.

A repair may be sufficient when: the roof is less than 15 years old, damage is isolated to a small area, and the overall structure is still sound.

Full replacement is the right choice when you see:

  • Your roof is 20+ years old and nearing or past its rated lifespan
  • Widespread damage covering more than 30% of the roof surface
  • Multiple active leak points throughout the home
  • Increasing energy bills caused by poor insulation and ventilation
  • Significant granule loss leaving shingles bare and brittle
  • Structural sagging or compromised roof decking
  • Storm or hail damage that voids manufacturer warranty coverage

Know the Warning Signs

Is Your Roof Due for Replacement?

San Antonio's intense summer heat, UV radiation, and seasonal hailstorms accelerate roof aging. Many homeowners don't realize their roof needs replacement until a serious leak causes expensive interior damage. Don't wait — learn the warning signs and act before a small problem becomes a major crisis.

  • Missing, curling, cracking, or buckling shingles across multiple areas
  • Daylight visible through the attic boards or rafters
  • Sagging or uneven areas indicating structural compromise
  • Storm or hail damage with visible dents, fractures, or bruising
  • Roof age exceeding 20–25 years for standard asphalt shingles
  • Dark streaks, moss, or algae growth across large sections
  • Flashing that is cracked, loose, or missing around chimneys and vents
